Keep Talking and Nobody Explodes Mod Fogey Me Not

On the Subject of Fogey Me Not

“These kids today with their shiny bosses.”

Fogey Me Not has ten buttons, each marked with a symbol, a submit button, labeled "●", a play button in the bottom right, and a display that shows the current stage number. The status light will light up with a random color (R, G, B, M, Y, C, W) each time the play button is pressed.

You may press any of the symbol buttons without causing a strike. The buttons start at 1 in the top left going to 10, in reading order. These are the digits you'll need to modify later on. For each symbol button press, instead of playing the regular button press sound, the module will play a random Keep Talking and Nobody Explodes vanilla sound. Also, the color of the status light will temporarily change to one of the previously mentioned colors, this will be the modification color for this stage.

Every time a new module is solved, you will move onto the next stage. For each stage, take the color of the status light that appears when you press the play button, and identify the sound it makes. Make sure you note down the sounds of the symbol buttons as soon as possible, as on some stages it will break and no sound will be played!

Using the status light color you got, you must modify the digit of the button that corresponds to the sound played when you pressed the play button. If it is Red, Green or Blue, you only need to modify the digit by that color rule on page 3. If it is a mixture of 2 or 3 colors, you must apply each of their individual rules. Note that if you need to modify a digit more than once, modify it in reading order of the tables on page 3.

You will also need to keep track of every color of status light that is shown (that is why in stage 1 the status light has a set color). Every time you get a new status light color, you must add it to the previous one (or the the starting color if on stage 1). Repeat this for each stage and when it's time to submit, press the R, G or B, buttons to turn the status light green. (The module will not show you the final status light color at the end).

Modifying the stage's digit

To add or subtract RGB components, you need to toggle them.

For example Magenta = R-B. Magenta + Blue = R--.


Red
Multiply your digit by the amount of letters in the serial number. Then take the square root of your new number and modulo 10, ignore any decimals. If your modified digit is 0 then add 10.
Green
Square your digit and then divide it by the number of batteries on the bomb (divide by 1 if there are no batteries) and ignore any decimals, modulo 10. If your modified digit is 0 then add 10.
Blue
Duplicate your digit so that is had a length of 6 (example: 444444). After every non-ignored module has been solved, Fogey Me Not will enter submission mode, you will know this when the display turns green and the there is no longer a stage number. There are three parts to submission mode. First is to press each button that corresponds to the modified digits you got from each stage.

When that is done correctly you must then press the R, G or B buttons to add to the status light color addition from each stage, to make the status light turn green. (If it was already green when the module entered submission mode you do not need to press any of the R, G or B buttons).

Finally, when you've entered the modified digits and status light color into the module correctly, you'll need to refer to the regular buttons again. The last thing you'll need to do is press the button who's sound played the most amount of times when pressing the play button on each stage. If there is a tie for the most played sound, use the next most played sound, until one is not tied.
